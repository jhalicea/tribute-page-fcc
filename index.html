Zen Master Thich Nhat Hanh

A global spiritual leader, poet and peace activist.

Here's A Timeline of Zen Master Thich Nhat Hanh:

  • 1926 - Born in Hue, Vietnam.
  • 1942 - At age 16 he started training with Zen Master Thanh Quy.
  • 1951 - Received full ordination as a Monk.
  • 1961 - Moves to the US to teach comparative religion at Princeton University, and was subsequently appointed lecturer in Buddhism at Columbia University.
  • 1963 - Return to Vietnam to aid his fellow monks in their non-violent peace efforts during the Vietnam War.
  • 1965 - Nhat Hanh writes a letter to Martin Luther King, Jr. entitled: "In Search of the Enemy of Man".
  • 1966 - Moves back to the US to lead a symposium in Vietnamese Buddhism at Cornell University, and to continue his work for peace. It was during his 1966 stay in the US that Thay met with King and urged him to publicly denounce the Vietnam War.
  • 1966 - Thay leave Vietnam for what he thought would be a few short weeks. It turned into forty years of exile, making France his new home.
  • 1967 - Dr. King nominates Thich Nhat Hanh for the 1967 Nobel Peace Prize. The fact that King had revealed the candidate he had chosen to nominate and had made a "strong request" to the prize committee, was in sharp violation of the Nobel traditions and protocol. The committee did not make an award that year.
  • 1969 - Thay establish The Plum Village Community. Creating monasteries around France and later the US.
  • 2005 - Following lengthy negotiations, Thay was given permission from the Vietnamese government to return home.
  • 2014 - Thay experienced a severe brain hemorrhage and was hospitalized.
  • 2018 - A press release from the Plum Village community confirmed that Nhat Hạnh, now aged 92, had returned to Vietnam a final time and will reside at Tu Hieu Temple to "live his remaining days".

– Thich Nhat Hanh
